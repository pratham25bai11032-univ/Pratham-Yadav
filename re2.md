## Introduction

Digital literacy is an essential skill for students because academic work, professional networking, collaboration, and financial transactions now happen through digital platforms every day. This project helped me understand that digital literacy is not limited to knowing how to use devices or apps. It also includes presenting oneself professionally online, communicating respectfully, verifying information, protecting personal data, and staying safe from cyber threats. Through the five tasks in this project, I worked on visual communication, digital portfolio building, coding-platform participation, email etiquette, and cybercrime awareness. Each module gave me practical experience with tools and habits that are useful not only for coursework but also for internships and long-term career development. This report documents the work completed in each module and reflects on what I learned from the activities.

## Module 1: Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic

For Module 1, I created a one-page infographic titled **"Digital Literacy: Your Guide to Navigating the Online World."** I used a visual design format to make the information simple, attractive, and easy for students to understand quickly. The infographic covers three major ideas from the brief: the meaning of digital literacy, useful digital tools for students, and safe internet practices. I included short explanations and supportive icons so that the poster would be informative without becoming text-heavy. The design uses soft academic colors and clear sectioning to guide the reader from concept to action. This task helped me realize that digital literacy awareness is most effective when information is presented in a structured and engaging way. While making the infographic, I focused on balancing creativity with clarity. I learned that visual communication is an important digital skill because a well-designed resource can spread awareness faster than a long block of text. The activity also improved my confidence in creating educational content for peers.

## Module 4: Professional Email and Online Etiquette

This module focused on professional communication and responsible online behavior. I drafted two formal emails: one requesting an assignment extension from a professor and another expressing interest in a summer internship opportunity. Writing these emails helped me understand the importance of a clear subject line, respectful greeting, concise explanation, and proper closing. I also prepared a list of social media do's and don'ts, which emphasized privacy, professionalism, fact-checking, emotional control, and respect for others. A common poor-communication scenario among students is sending rushed messages without a subject line, greeting, or context, especially when asking for help at the last minute. Such communication can appear careless and may reduce the chance of receiving a positive response. This module taught me that tone, structure, and timing matter just as much as content in digital communication.

**Artifacts:** `Email_ettiquite.txt`, `e2.txt`, `5 do and dont.txt`

## Module 5: Cybercrime Awareness

For Module 5, I worked on a case study about UPI and online payment fraud, a cybercrime that is highly relevant to students and families in India. The case study explains how scammers misuse trust and confusion around collect requests, OTPs, and UPI PINs to steal money. I also created a prevention checklist with practical safety actions such as enabling two-factor authentication, reviewing permissions, avoiding suspicious links, verifying UPI requests, and reporting incidents quickly through the National Cyber Crime Portal or helpline 1930. This module was especially valuable because cyber awareness is not only technical knowledge but also a daily-life responsibility. Many online frauds succeed because users act in haste or without verification. After completing this task, I became more conscious about digital caution, especially in financial matters, public Wi-Fi use, and phishing attempts.

**Artifacts:** `casestudy.md`, `prevention-checklist.md`
